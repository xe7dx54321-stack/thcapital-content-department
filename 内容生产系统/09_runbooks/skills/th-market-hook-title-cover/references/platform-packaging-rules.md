# Platform Packaging Rules

This file defines working rules for packaging TH Capital public content across platforms.

These are **internal operating heuristics**, not platform-official thresholds.

## 1. WeChat

### Packaging goal

- earn the click
- reduce early confusion
- keep the reader in the article long enough to reach the real argument

### Title rules

- say what the reader gets, not just what happened
- keep one main contradiction or one clear result
- avoid stacking too many nouns
- avoid writing like a newsletter subject line with no object

### Cover rules

- one sharp message per cover
- make the conflict or payoff visible
- do not overload with abstract terms

### Opening rules

- hook first
- context bridge early
- main judgment visible before the article feels “slow”

---

## 2. Xiaohongshu

### Packaging goal

- stop scroll fast
- make the topic feel close to the user
- lower the understanding threshold

### Title rules

- plain language wins over elegant abstraction
- user-benefit beats industry jargon
- make the stake obvious

### Cover rules

- the reader should know in one glance:
  - what this is
  - why it matters
  - why they should care now

### Opening rules

- start with user impact
- translate the topic fast
- move the background into everyday language

---

## 3. Zhihu

### Packaging goal

- answer a real question
- support search intent
- win trust through clarity

### Title rules

- package like a clear question or answer
- imply the controversy, misunderstanding, or judgment
- avoid “朋友圈体” phrasing

### Opening rules

- answer first
- then add context
- then expand with reasoning

---

## 4. X

### Packaging goal

- make the first line independently valuable
- trigger response, saves, reposts, or thread continuation

### Title / opening rules

- the first line is the package
- one claim only
- do not spend the first line on setup

### Thread rules

- line 1 = claim
- line 2 = why now / why interesting
- line 3 onward = explanation or evidence

---

## 5. Cross-platform anti-failure rules

If CTR is weak, suspect:

- the title is too vague
- the cover is too abstract
- the stake is not visible

If readers click but do not continue, suspect:

- the promise and body do not match
- the article delays context too long
- the opening is clever but not informative

If readers understand but do not care, suspect:

- the user stake is too weak
- the argument is true but not useful
- the packaging shows “what happened” but not “why this matters”
