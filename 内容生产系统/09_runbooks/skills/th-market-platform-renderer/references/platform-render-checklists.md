# Platform Render Checklists

Use these checklists before marking a rendered asset as ready for publish queue.

## 1. WeChat checklist

- Is the first-screen package clear?
- Is there an optional lead / subtitle if needed?
- Are paragraphs short enough for mobile reading?
- Are key judgments called out as quote / emphasis candidates?
- Are image or divider insertion points visible?
- Are source / risk notes retained?

## 2. Xiaohongshu checklist

- Does the cover say one clear thing?
- Is page 1 a conclusion, not setup?
- Are cards short, visual, and individually legible?
- Is the topic translated into plain language?
- Is the last card interaction / CTA clear?

## 3. Zhihu checklist

- Is the answer visible before long explanation begins?
- Is the background bridge compact?
- Are lists / sections clearer than the raw draft?
- Are claims and evidence still aligned?

## 4. X checklist

- Can line 1 survive on its own?
- Is the object clear by line 1 or line 2?
- Does each post add one piece of information?
- Is the final line worth replying to or saving?

## 5. Bilibili checklist

- Does the opening promise a practical takeaway?
- Are steps / modules / pitfalls visible at a glance?
- Does the piece feel like a real builder write-up rather than a generic article?
- Are examples or concrete failure conditions preserved?

## 6. Toutiao checklist

- Does the headline earn the click without semantic drift?
- Does the first paragraph pay off fast enough?
- Is the language more大众化 than the master draft?
- Does the body still preserve the real judgment and risk note?

## 7. Baijiahao checklist

- Does the title match real search intent?
- Is the answer visible early enough for search readers?
- Are headings keyword-friendly without obvious stuffing?
- Are source / risk notes preserved without breaking readability?

## 8. Global checklist

- Does this feel native to the platform?
- Can the human publisher execute without guessing?
- Is any platform-specific output still pretending to be more complete than it is?
