# Audience Layers

Use this file to decide which audience a TH Capital content asset is really serving.

## Layer A｜AI-curious beginner / one-person-company aspirer

### What they already know

- AI is important
- agent is hot
- there are many new tools and products

### What they usually do not know

- technical stack details
- infrastructure tradeoffs
- why one industry event matters more than another

### What they most want

- clear explanation
- relevance to themselves
- a sense of direction or opportunity

### Writing implications

- define the object fast
- explain jargon immediately
- use concrete examples
- emphasize “why this matters to you”

---

## Layer B｜Tech operator / creator / builder-adjacent reader

### What they already know

- basic AI vocabulary
- rough product categories
- some workflow pain

### What they most want

- how this changes execution
- what the key variable is
- what can actually be used or copied

### Writing implications

- less glossary, more mechanism
- explain workflow consequences
- point out where the edge or bottleneck is

---

## Layer C｜Business / investment / strategic reader

### What they already know

- product categories
- market narratives
- some platform and business dynamics

### What they most want

- where value accrues
- whether this is structural or transient
- how the industry map may change

### Writing implications

- move quickly from event to structure
- discuss distribution, market power, business model, ecosystem consequence
- avoid teaching basics too slowly

---

## Mixed-layer rule

Some platform versions can serve two layers.

Typical pairs:

- WeChat: `A/B` or `B/C`
- Xiaohongshu: `A` with a little `B`
- Zhihu: `B` or `B/C`
- X: usually one sharp layer only
